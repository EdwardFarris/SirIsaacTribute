The Highlights of Newton's Life

  • 1642-A Merry Christmas for Science

    • Isaac Newton is born on December 25th in Woolsthorpe, England. As an infant, Newton was weak and almost died, but thankfully pulled through.

  • 1661-Time for School

    • At 16 years old, He enrolls at Trinity College at Cambridge.

  • 1665-Shooting for the Stars

    • Newton receives his bachelor's of arts degree from Cambridge.

  • 1666-School's Out for Summer...I Mean Plague

    • Cambridge closes down because of the plague in London. Back home, he experiments with the origin of colors and the nature of gravity, and creates what we know as calculus today.

  • 1667-From Student to Teacher

    • Cambridge reopens and Newton returns to study for his master of arts degree. In 1669 he is made a professor of mathematics.

  • 1671-Onward and Upward

    • Newton builds an improved version of the reflecting telescope. His friend, Dr. Barrow, takes it to the Royal Society of London and they invite Newton to join.

  • 1687-Laws of the Universe

    • His work on planetary motion turns into a 3-volume book known as Principia. This work details the 3 laws of motion and the law of universal gravitation.

  • 1689-Order in the Court

    • Newton runs for Parliament and wins a seat. He serves for one year.

  • 1693-Possible Mercury Poisoning

    • Newton's health begins to fail and his mind seems to wander, but is completely well by 1696.

  • 1696-Show Me the Money

    • Newton is given the job of Warden of the Mint and is eventually appointed Master of the Mint in 1700.

  • 1703-A Bright Light

    • Newton is elected the president of the Royal Society of London and publishes a book about light called Opticks.

  • 1705-A Seat at the Round Table

    • In 1705 Newton receives the highest honor in the land. He becomes the first British scientist to be knighted

  • March 20, 1727-Farewell, Good Knight

    • Sir Isaac Newton dies at the age of 84. He is buried at the famous Westminster Abbey.

"If I have seen further than others, it is by standing upon the shoulders of giants." -Sir Isaac Newton
